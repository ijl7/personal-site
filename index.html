Site Info

This site was primarily built as a passion project, in order to hone my skills and also show expansion into new areas, since as previously mentioned, many school projects are more focused on back-end technology. However, this website was not simply built as resume padding. It further allowed me to see how managing my time on a project would work, and also allowed for building of my creativity and problem solving, as I attempted to incorporate features that are both aesthetically pleasing and useful. Building this site also involved a fair amount of research, not only into new coding languages, but how to purchase a domain and manage the site as well.

When I began building my site, I quickly realized that on one service, all I would be allowed to do was to build a static site. That may be sufficient for a site like this that holds a portfolio, but I wanted to do more. I had to install new tools and go to a new provider in order to make the site dynamic. I felt as if this was a necessary part of my development in web programming, since many sites nowadays use dynamic sites in order to customize things like ads based on who is visiting the site.

I then purchased a domain name and generated a CNAME record for my newly purchased domain on the service. This is logged to ensure that I own the domain. Finally, I took extra steps to make sure my website was secure. I generated a SSL certificate to make sure that all traffic was protected from attacks. This Transport Layer Security allows for peace of mind when browsing through my site, even though I may not be peddling any goods besides my hard work.

Now, you might be wondering why the site has a password-protected section. The idea for this began when my friends (the Double Take Video ones) told me that I could help them coding a website for online purchases. After filming the Streetsboro Bicentennial, citizens were allowed to purchase a video, with one option being for a digital copy. Building my website first allowed me to focus on some things that might be necessary for a site where clients can enter their code and get the video. In this case, I used my clips from Valorant, and I opened a viewer instead of a download link. The username is admin and the password is password.
